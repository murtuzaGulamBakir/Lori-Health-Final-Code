Intermittent Fasting

Frequently Asked Questions

Basics

Lori Intermittent Fasting Tracker

Intermittent fasting is not a diet but is a method of eating. It falls in the concept of “not only what you eat is important, but also when you eat is!”. The fasting method helps body undergo various processes and help with efficient breakdown of stored fats. The method is scientifically proven and has tons of research attached to it.

You can start as a beginner with the basic fast cycle. The fasting tracker reminds you and keeps you posted as to when to start and end the fast and what is happening with your body while you fast! And your health plan is altered to accommodate your fasting cycle, so you know what you should eat when you stop the fast.

Truthfully, everybody responds to fasting a little differently.

We should advice that intermittent fasting isn’t for everyone. In case of an existing clinical condition, please consult with your physician if it is advisable for you to be on one

Intermittent Fasting is suitable for adults, based on scientific research. It is not advisable for those in periods of rapid growth, such as children and adolescents.

If you are pregnant or breastfeeding, intermittent fasting is not advisable for you.

Anyone with a chronic disease or elderly shouldn’t commence intermittent fasting without guidance. If you have an existing clinical concern, please consult with your physician to understand the risks of intermittent fasting, should you have any.

On the fasting tracker, you can see a dropdown to select a fasting cycle. You can use the below guide to select the one that suits you.

If you a newbie, start slowly, with a 12-14 hour fast. This fast is relatively easy to follow as you probably are doing it without realising it – e.g. If you take your dinner at 8PM and the next day breakfast at 8AM, this indicates a 12 hour fast, if you don’t consume anything else in this window. This cycle will help with general detox and shows steady progress over time. You can do this all your life, without any issues.

If you are familiar with fasting, you can choose a 16-18 hour fast. You can manage this with a little effort. Think of a regime where you eat a dinner at 8pm and the next meal at 12pm the next day. Because of the longer fast cycle, this fast has more benefits.

If you are already an expert, go ahead with a 20+ hour fast to push yourself a bit further. Because your gut rests for an extended period of time, it makes you feel more energetic and focused. Despite the many benefits, we don’t recommend you do this every day.

Bottomline, giving your gut rest leads to many health benefits!

While you are fasting, you should drink plenty of water to make sure you are not dehydrated. You can also have herbal teas (e.g., green tea) or a black coffee (without any sweetener or sugar). Do not consume any sweeteners or their substitutes and zero calorie drinks

Intermittent Fasting has proven effective in controlling the spikes in blood sugar. But, before you start a fasting regime, please consult with your physician.

While breastfeeding, you would require additional calories not fewer to support the milk production. We do not advise breastfeeding mothers to do an intermittent fasting regime.

Intermittent Fasting has proven effective in controlling the spikes in blood sugar. But, before you start a fasting regime, please consult with your physician.
