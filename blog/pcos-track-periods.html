PCOS and tracking your periods: Does that help?

YES, It does! Tracking your periods can help you a lot and be that one friend that never lets you down! PCOS/PCOD is a hormonal imbalance disorder that predominantly attacks your ovaries and creates complications in your menstruation and ovulation process. Therefore, tracking your periods can help you detect PCOS and also help in keeping tabs on your PCOS symptoms.

[READ MORE: WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF PCOS?]

Can you detect PCOS by tracking periods?

When you have PCOS/PCOD, your body will have higher male hormone levels in your ovaries and it will create small cysts in the entrance of your ovaries to block the release of your eggs. This will cause a lot of irregularities in your menstrual cycle. Therefore, by tracking your periods you can detect PCOS symptoms early on and find ways to reverse your PCOS. It is vital for you to detect PCOS at an earlier stage because, at a later stage, it can lead to serious health conditions such as Endometrial/Uterine cancer and Cardiovascular diseases.

What does PCOS period tracking mean?

PCOS can be initially detected through its major symptom, the irregularity in the menstrual cycle. This irregularity varies from women to women so merely marking your periods on a calendar will do you no good. You need to observe your menstrual cycle for over a year and calculate the no of days between each menstruation in order to figure out if you really have irregularities in your periods because of PCOS. The average duration between each menstruation is 28 days but anything between 21 and 35 days is also considered to be in the normal range so along with this, tracking your periods for a longer time is also necessary. Some of the commonly experienced irregularities faced by women with PCOS are:

  • Delay in getting the first periods in Adolescents.
  • Having less than 8 periods in a year
  • Having no periods for 2-3 consecutive months
  • Excessive bleeding ( more than 80 ml blood) along with painful cramps in the pelvic region.
  • Periods lasting for less than 2 days or lasting more than 5 days.

How does tracking periods help after diagnosing PCOS?

Women, who are diagnosed with PCOS, commonly worry about their fertility since PCOS affects the quality of the eggs produced in ovaries, which leads to infertility and multiple miscarriages. Once you are diagnosed with PCOS and start your reversal of PCOS process, your periods will start to get back on track But you still need to continue tracking your periods to get familiar with your body’s fertility period. Usually, the time frame between your ovulation (release of egg from ovary) and your period starting is 6 days so the five days in between is best to try conceiving. Since your PCOS will still continue to have some effect on your periods, tracking your periods regularly will help you figure out the pattern in your periods and determine that 5-day window that’s best to get pregnant.

Even if you are not trying to get pregnant, tracking your periods can be of great help. You can use your period info to determine if your current treatment towards the reversal of PCOS is effective and help you track the improvement of your PCOS symptoms. Having definite evidence of your periods getting regular can help raise your spirits and eventually help win your battle against PCOS.
