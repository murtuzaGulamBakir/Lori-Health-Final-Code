Do I have to lose weight to reverse PCOS/PCOD?

The answer is YES! Weight loss can significantly help you in reversing PCOS. Polycystic Ovary Syndrome is a hormone imbalance syndrome, which increases the Androgen (Male hormone) and insulin levels in your body. These excess levels will create multiple cysts in your ovaries and disrupt your menstrual cycle and create hormone-related issues such as acne and excessive body hairs.

These symptoms can lead to long-term health issues such as Type 2 Diabetes and Uterine cancer. When you are overweight, the overall risk of these serious health issues increases exponentially. Therefore losing weight is the best option to reduce the overall risk and reverse your PCOS symptoms.

What is the link between PCOS and obesity?

When you have PCOS, you will develop a condition called Insulin resistance and, your body will resist the insulin present, forcing your pancreas to secrete an excessive amount of insulin. This causes a fluctuation in your Blood sugar levels and increases the secretion of Androgens in your ovaries. Furthermore, the energy from your food will be stored as fat and makes you obese. The more weight you gain, the more your other symptoms will start to develop at a quicker pace.

How can losing weight help reverse PCOS?

Weight loss helps restore the normal functioning of your ovaries and stabilizes the hormone production in your body. Researchers say that reducing weight by 5-10% can reverse the following aspects of your PCOS symptoms:

  • Reduces the insulin resistance in your body by 50%
  • Stops the excess secretion of Androgens (male hormones)
  • Help reduce acne and excessive body hairs.
  • Regulates your menstrual cycle.
  • Removes cysts in your ovaries and restores seamless ovulation.
  • Improves your egg fertilization and reduces complications in pregnancy.
  • Decreases the risk of developing serious health issues like Type 2 Diabetes and heart-related diseases.

How can PCOS women reduce weight?

The best way to reduce weight is by making lifestyle changes in your life. The two major aspects you need to work on are healthy food intake and doing more physical activity. Making sudden changes in your life can be very difficult but, if you make reversing your PCOS your goal and work with perseverance, you will definitely reverse your PCOS.

Can exercise help with PCOS reversal?

The lack of physical activity is one of the contributing factors to insulin resistance. In order to lose, weight physical activity and exercise are very crucial. Experts suggest that a minimum of 150 min of physical activity per week is optimal for reversing PCOS. Make sure to include a workout routine in your everyday plans and commit to it. This will increase your metabolism and maintain your hormone levels.

How to plan your diet to reverse PCOS?

There are few things that you need to keep in mind while planning your diet. For an effective weight loss and reversal of PCOS, you need to make sure that your food should include:

  • Lesser carbohydrates to reduce your insulin level.
  • High fibre-rich food in order to increase your body’s response to insulin.
  • More proteins to stabilize your Blood sugar levels.
  • Avoid processed food and added sugars.
